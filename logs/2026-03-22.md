### Headline

Resolv's USR stablecoin was exploited for ~$80M through a flaw in `completeSwap()`, minting unbacked USR and depegging the asset to $0.257 (~74% drop), with ~$23.8M already swapped into ETH at attacker wallet `0x8ED8cF0C1c531C1b20848E78f1CB32fa5B99b81C`.

### Incidents

- **Resolv USR (depeg, critical)**: Preetam (QuillAudits) broke the alert: `completeSwap()` let the attacker mint $80M USR without draining collateral, with no hardcoded supply cap. Attacker address `0x04A288a7789DD6Ade935361a4fB1Ec5db513caEd`; ~$23.8M of the proceeds sat at `0x8ED8cF0C1c531C1b20848E78f1CB32fa5B99b81C`. Popeye collated cross-protocol "limited exposure" disclosures ([Resolv](https://x.com/resolvlabs/status/2035581976514609437), [Gauntlet](https://x.com/gauntlet_xyz/status/2035588296592789560), [Venus](https://x.com/venusprotocol/status/2035579052317438340)). Mikko asked why the attacker stopped at $80M when there were no hardcoded limits; Teddy and 0xAnon clarified the cap wasn't a mint limit but a liquidity limit (you can only extract what's in the pools). Anmol recommended threshold sigs for automated tx signing.
- **Google Chrome critical update (infrastructure, high)**: Pablo flagged a forced-update push covering 26 CVEs, three critical: `CVE-2026-4439` (WebGL OOB memory access), `CVE-2026-4440` (WebGL OOB read/write), `CVE-2026-4441` (Base use-after-free). Update path: `Settings > About Chrome > Update and relaunch`.

### Headline

Mikko surfaced Transak's KYC data leak via VECERT Radar (an older 2024 breach resurfacing), Odysseas diagnosed Denaria Finance's exploit as coming from a vector outside their defined invariant set, and NonseOdion dropped Part 1 of his Balancer-hack series from the attacker perspective.

### Incidents

- **Transak KYC leak (infrastructure, med)**: Mikko shared [VECERT Radar tweet](https://x.com/VECERTRadar/status/2040019818582044770). Popeye added context: "Collection of older data breach in 2024 (phishing incident) that exposed 92,554 users' IDs and selfies via a third-party vendor."
- **Denaria Finance (exploit, low)**: Arseniy noted "I remember Linea deployed a sequencer-level firewall some time ago, looks like Denaria didn't deploy any invariants there." Duncan: "0x has been using this to mitigate the impact of 3rd party mistakes with our contracts..." Odysseas (Phylax): "yeah, the attack was from a vector for which invariants had not been defined. Invariant mapping is the most challenging of the process, as once a protocol has done it, then enforcing them is very easy. We have been thinking a lot on how to make it easier for protocols, by offering templates across protocol families, as also generic circuit breaking." Duncan plugged [Phylax](https://phylax.systems/).

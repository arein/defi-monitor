### Headline

Arbitrum's Security Council executed a 9-of-12 L1 forced transaction that upgraded the rollup contracts, rescued roughly $70M of KelpDAO exploit funds from the attacker's Arbitrum wallet, and reverted the upgrade in the same tx, prompting a loud debate about what "L2 security" actually means.

### Incidents

- **Arbitrum Security Council rescue (infrastructure, high)**: a 9-of-12 council multisig pushed an ArbOS/inbox upgrade, issued a forced transaction (tx type 101) to impersonate the exploiter and move funds, then reverted the implementation in the same L1 transaction. Odysseas (Phylax) gave the [canonical decomposition](https://etherscan.io/tx/0x079984c56c5670108f5c6f664904178f9b364340351949a42e4637d1f645f770) of the three-step flow; banteg noted it required a coordinated hardfork and ArbOS upgrade, not a standard emergency path. Official writeup: [Arbitrum forum post](https://forum.arbitrum.foundation/t/security-council-emergency-action-21-04-2026/30803). 0x44 argued the action proves L2s are not decentralized as advertised; James Prestwich countered that Ethereum and Bitcoin have both clawed back tokens before, so the discomfort is ideological, not novel. heraldur pointed out the attacker would have escaped recovery by swapping to USDC on Arbitrum, making this a Circle-dependent outcome in parallel.
- **Thetanuts Finance (exploit, low)**: Hawre reported a ~$46K drain of the Cash Settled Covered Call BTC/USD vault via a Morpho-sourced 10 WBTC flash loan. The attacker deposited 2 satoshis to claim 1 share under the vault's rounding-down share math, then a larger 4.68 WBTC deposit, then called `initWithdraw()` with a max value to burn all shares and walk away with the vault's entire 4.83 WBTC balance (net ~0.1518 WBTC after loan repay). Vault contract was unverified ([address](https://etherscan.io/address/0x80b8eeb34a2ba5dd90c61e02a12ea30515dca6f5)); Icalurus published a [PoC repo](https://github.com/DK27ss/Thetanuts-46k-PoC).

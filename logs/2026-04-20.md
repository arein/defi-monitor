### Headline

The LayerZero post-mortem for Kelp rsETH landed and the channel spent the day pulling it apart: LZ attributes the compromise to an RPC-spoofing attack where the attacker swapped op-geth binaries on two independent Unichain RPC nodes feeding the DVN, shifting the narrative from "1/1 DVN was the root cause" to "even 2/2 and 3/3 would have been compromised if they all read the same RPC surface."

### Incidents

- **Kelp DAO / LayerZero (post-mortem, ongoing high-severity infra)**: the official LZ write-up (shared by banteg, Insomniac, and Danger | TiD — [banteg thread](https://x.com/banteg/status/2046124661352644907)) claims the attacker gained shell-level access to two Unichain op-geth nodes used by the DVN and spoofed RPC responses. banteg adds that [LZ publicly exposes its RPC providers via a health-check endpoint](https://gist.github.com/banteg/2fde29d381f8f261b57c974417b36b48) and notes LZ verified Merkle proofs in ULNv1 but stopped verifying them in ULNv2 (James Prestwich confirms this matches his 2019-era disclosure about LZ being able to swap the inbound proof library). Marco (TAC) makes the day's sharpest point: abandoned chains like Unichain (where DIN disconnected RPC services a month ago) are a critical systemic risk because the RPC pool is small enough to compromise across any reasonable DVN quorum. Lore (Cap) disputes this, arguing compromising RPCs across multiple operators and waiting for finality raises the cost exponentially. USDT0 and others remain paused awaiting confirmation that L0 as a whole is safe; m4rio notes no official all-clear yet.
- **Vercel compromise (supply chain, scope unclear)**: Angry_Ninja_Dev, Danger | TiD, and Vladimir Officercia flagged an apparent Vercel breach; unclear whether env variables or customer projects were affected. Off-topic for the channel's core focus but material for anyone deploying frontends there. Vladimir shared his [mitigation thread](https://x.com/officer_secret/status/2045973049841967561).

### Discussion

Doggie B ran a long thread on why native rollup bridges avoid this class of attack: rollup actions flow through L1 settlement with cryptographic proofs, while cross-chain messaging layers like LZ traded settlement security for UX and don't track bridged supply as a settled ledger on the issuance chain. Marco (TAC) and Lore (Cap) pushed the conclusion: drop optimistic bridging, wait for finality, and lean on zkVMs now that they are viable. Odysseas (Phylax) cited [the 2019 Zellic paper on bridge design schools](https://eprint.iacr.org/2019/1128) as the canonical reference. Opsec pointer: Dickson surfaced the [SEAL frameworks site](https://frameworks.securityalliance.org) when Danger asked for a consolidated opsec checklist.

Also mentioned: an admin-call flurry and James asking fubuloubu to ban him as a joke; a reference to Curvance's older hack; Anmol's [QuillAudits summary of the Kelp flow](https://x.com/QuillAudits_AI/status/2046248234671284388); a quiet reminder from Odysseas that "using Binance as a bridge stays undefeated."

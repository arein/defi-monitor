### Headline

Pablo (Sabbatella) warned of a new global wave of Telegram account takeovers via SMS auth codes intercepted at the SMS-tier provider level (between Twilio and mobile operators), and Gery's running callout of `cryptosweb3ledger[.]com` phishing domain exposed long-standing takedown friction.

### Incidents

- **Telegram SMS takeovers (phishing, high)**: Pablo: "We suspect SMS with auth codes sent by Twilio are being intercepted at the tier level, like happened in February last year... LATAM for sure (argentina, uruguay, chile and brasil)." Between Twilio and mobile operators, "tiers" route SMS; those companies have weak security and their admin logins often appear in leaked databases. Mitigation: `Settings > Privacy and Security > Two-Step verification: turn it on, write it down and configure a secure email` ([full guide](https://x.com/PabloSabbatella/status/2029203722270630313)). Anatol connected to a recent Romanian Orange pattern; Pablo confirmed it's not always the operator (tier provider is the real weak point).
- **Ledger phishing domain (phishing, med)**: Gery reported `cryptosweb3ledger[.]com` has been live for 6+ months. blackbigswan (SEAL) explained Metamask blocks it since October 2025 but without Metamask / some wallets, users get no phish warning. Gery's sharper critique: "Ledger doesn't care about customers who lost funds and doesn't care about taking down sites impersonating them." Tay joined the pile-on: "if you have the ability to take shit down why haven't you already." Debate evolved into whether Ledger has a business incentive to take it down (Gery: no, since wallet-drained users stop paying subs).
